## Key Insights

### 1. Momentum Gap-Up vs Fragile Gap-Up ŌĆö CRITICAL CLASSIFICATION

This is the **first gap-up session where morning beat afternoon**, breaking a
3-session pattern (Jun 24, 27, 29). The critical distinction:

| Feature | Fragile Gap-Up (Jun 24/27/29) | Momentum Gap-Up (Jun 30) |
|---------|-------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Leading sector | Homebuilders (ITB), mixed, crossover | Pure semiconductors (XLK) |
| Sector excess | Mixed, <1.5% | XLK +1.71% |
| Top stock type | Homebuilder, mixed cap | Large-cap tech/semi |
| Morning 5minŌåÆClose | +0.01%, +0.01%, -0.54% | +1.32% |
| Afternoon 5minŌåÆClose | +0.89%, +0.63%, +0.31% | -0.18% |
| AM vs PM reference | PM better | AM better (3 of 5) |
| Key dynamic | Opening euphoria fades | Momentum sustains through close |

**Rule: Not all gap-up days are created equal.** The character of the leading
sector determines whether morning peaks fade or sustain.

### 2. Afternoon Picks Diluted Returns

3 new afternoon-only picks (JCI, FAST, ROKU) were all negative ŌĆö two from XLI
(the weaker sector with only +0.30% excess) and one from XOVR. Meanwhile the
morning semiconductor stalwarts continued performing. Afternoon should prioritize
re-evaluating morning momentum names over introducing new picks from weaker sectors.

### 3. Sector Quality Gate

XLI (+0.30% excess) couldn't lift individual stocks: morning -0.33%, afternoon -0.65%.
XLK (+1.71% excess) delivered: morning +2.42%, afternoon +0.24%. A minimum 0.5%
excess threshold would have excluded XLI, improving concentration.

### 4. 10:30 Check ŌĆö 4th Consecutive Working Signal

HWM was the only stock below the 5-min average at 10:30 (-1.53%) and was the
only morning loser (-1.54% to close). The 10:30 check has now correctly flagged
the worst performer in 4 consecutive sessions (Jun 25 PHM, Jun 27, Jun 29 TTWO,

### 5. Building Momentum (RSI <60) Fails on Strong Days

JCI (RSI 55.5) was the only building-momentum pick and was the worst PM performer
(-0.93%). On strong momentum days, money flows to already-moving names. RSI <60
should be penalized in scoring on strong trend days (VOO >+0.5%, sector >1.5%).

### 6. Repeated Pick: AMD Morning Was the Real Opportunity

AMD picked in both sessions. Morning entry +4.63% vs afternoon +0.21%.
Morning scouting identified the star; afternoon entry added minimal value at
a +4.4% higher price. Repeated picks should note morning-entry superiority.
